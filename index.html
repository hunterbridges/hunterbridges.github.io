Hunter Bridges the Musician

I've been playing and writing music since I was a child. It is my deepest passion, and something I can't live without.

I grew up in Birmingham, AL playing a lot of video games. Through this I became influenced by the great pantheon of video game composers, notably Koji Kondo (Nintendo), Nobuo Uematsu (Final Fantasy), Yoko Shimomura (Street Fighter II), Jun Senoue (Sonic the Hedgehog), Junichi Nakatsuru (Soul Calibur) and Motoi Sakuraba (Golden Sun). During high school I nerded out on prog rock bands like Rush, The Fall of Troy, The Mars Volta, and Coheed and Cambria.

In 2011 I graduated from Belmont University in Nashville, TN with a Bachelor of Music (B.M.) in Commercial Music. My primary instrument was drum set, and my degree emphasis was in music technology.

After college I moved to San Francisco, CA, and now I live in Los Angeles, CA.

I've prepared a selection of pieces to showcase here. If you'd like to hear more, visit my SoundCloud. If you'd like to purchase any of my albums, I encourage you to do so on Bandcamp. Like what you hear? !

Nexus Genesis is an album of 16-bit FM synth tunes I recently released. It was originally written for a Sonic fan-game. Check out some gameplay footage:

First Flight of the Airship and Longing are examples of my orchestration abilities. They draw upon influences from cinematic scores and role playing games.

I like to do cover tunes to experiment with arranging and production techniques.

And sometimes I drop all the way down to 8-bit because I love letting limitations inspire my creativity!

Hunter Bridges the Programmer

I've always been fascinated with computers. I believe they are an amazing medium for creativity, especially considering my love of video games. I started learning HTML in elementary school, and picked up PHP in middle school. I took high school classes in Visual Basic and Java.

In college, I paid rent by doing web development freelancing. Soon after the release of the App Store, I picked up a job creating iOS apps for a company in Nashville. Little did I know, I was bringing a significant part of my career to life.

In 2011, after I graduated college, I moved to San Francisco and got mixed up in the tech industry. Since then, I've mainly worked as an iOS developer, but have also done plenty of web and server programming.

I'm productive in most of today's relevant programming languages: Swift, Objective-C, Ruby, Python, JavaScript, Lua, C, C++, and C#. I'm also well-versed in principles of product design and interaction design.

I'm currently based in Los Angeles, CA, working as a freelancer. Want to hire me for a contract? !

I am currently working with Maiden Lane and Collective Health.

From 2013 to 2015 I was working at Jawbone as a Research and Development Engineer. I primarily explored using binaural spatial audio in various augmented reality contexts.

From 2011 to 2013 I worked at Wantful (now defunct). I was responsible for leading the development of Wantful for iPad, which was featured as an Editor's Choice pick on the App Store. The app pioneered many UI design conventions now common today.

From 2009 to 2011 I worked at Aloompa in Nashville, TN creating iPhone apps for music festivals.

I've also held various engagements with other companies as a freelancer.
